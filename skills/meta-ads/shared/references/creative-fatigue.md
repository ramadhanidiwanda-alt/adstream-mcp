# Creative Fatigue Diagnosis

Creative fatigue happens when your audience has seen your ad too many times and stops engaging. It's the #1 reason CPM rises and ROAS falls on Meta.

## Symptoms

**Classic fatigue signature (all 3 must be present):**
1. Frequency > 3.0 (cold prospecting) or > 4.5 (retargeting)
2. CPM rising ≥30% week-over-week
3. Link CTR falling ≥20% week-over-week

**Secondary indicators:**
- Hook rate declining (video ads)
- Cost per action rising while spend is flat
- Impressions growing but reach plateauing

## Diagnosis Matrix

| Frequency | CPM Trend | CTR Trend | Diagnosis |
|-----------|-----------|-----------|-----------|
| > 3.0 | Rising 30%+ | Falling 20%+ | **Creative fatigue** |
| > 3.0 | Rising 30%+ | Stable | Audience saturation |
| < 3.0 | Rising 30%+ | Falling 20%+ | Auction pressure (competitors) |
| > 3.0 | Stable | Falling 20%+ | Creative worn out (refresh) |
| > 3.0 | Rising 30%+ | Stable/rising | Audience too narrow |

## When to Refresh Creative

**Immediate refresh (within 48 hours):**
- Frequency > 4.0 on cold prospecting
- CPM up 50%+ in one week
- CTR down 40%+ in one week
- ROAS dropped below break-even

**Planned refresh (within 1 week):**
- Frequency 3.0-4.0 on cold prospecting
- CPM up 30-50% over 2 weeks
- CTR down 20-40% over 2 weeks

**Monitor closely (refresh in 2-4 weeks):**
- Frequency 2.5-3.0 on cold prospecting
- CPM up 15-30% over 4 weeks
- CTR down 10-20% over 4 weeks

## What NOT to Do

❌ **Don't increase budget** — this accelerates fatigue
❌ **Don't pause the ad set** — you lose learning data
❌ **Don't change targeting** — this resets learning phase
❌ **Don't lower bids** — this doesn't fix creative fatigue

## What to Do

✅ **Refresh creative** — new image/video, new copy, new angle
✅ **Rotate to fresh audience** — new lookalike seed, broader targeting
✅ **Duplicate ad set with new creative** — keep old one running at reduced budget
✅ **Test new format** — if using static image, try video; if using video, try carousel

## Creative Refresh Checklist

When recommending creative refresh, specify:

1. **Which ad sets** — name them specifically
2. **Evidence** — cite frequency, CPM trend, CTR trend
3. **Dollar impact** — how much is fatigue costing per day/month
4. **Refresh strategy** — what type of creative to test next
5. **Timeline** — how urgent (immediate vs planned)

**Example recommendation:**

> **Campaign: Summer Sale 2026**
> **Ad Set: LAL 1% Purchasers**
> 
> **Fatigue detected:**
> - Frequency: 3.8 (up from 2.1 two weeks ago)
> - CPM: $42 (up 55% from $27)
> - Link CTR: 0.9% (down 35% from 1.4%)
> - ROAS: 1.8x (down from 3.2x)
> 
> **Impact:** Losing $340/week vs 2 weeks ago
> 
> **Action:** Immediate creative refresh (within 48 hours)
> - Current creative: Static product image with discount callout
> - Test next: UGC-style video testimonial or before/after demonstration
> - Keep current ad set running at 50% budget while new creative learns

## Frequency Guidelines by Audience Type

| Audience Type | Healthy | Warning | Critical |
|---------------|---------|---------|----------|
| Cold prospecting (broad/interest) | < 2.0 | 2.0-3.0 | > 3.0 |
| Lookalike 1-3% | < 2.5 | 2.5-3.5 | > 3.5 |
| Lookalike 4-10% | < 2.0 | 2.0-3.0 | > 3.0 |
| Custom audience (engagers) | < 3.0 | 3.0-4.0 | > 4.0 |
| Retargeting (website visitors) | < 3.5 | 3.5-5.0 | > 5.0 |
| Retargeting (cart abandoners) | < 4.0 | 4.0-6.0 | > 6.0 |

Warmer audiences can tolerate higher frequency because they already know the brand.

## Creative Rotation Strategy

**For ongoing campaigns (always-on):**
- Rotate creative every 2-4 weeks proactively
- Don't wait for fatigue to set in
- Keep 2-3 creatives in rotation per ad set
- Pause lowest performer when adding new creative

**For seasonal/promotional campaigns:**
- Plan 3-5 creative variations upfront
- Rotate every 7-14 days during campaign
- Monitor frequency daily during high-spend periods

**For testing campaigns:**
- Test 3-5 creatives simultaneously
- Let them run until frequency hits 2.5
- Kill bottom 50%, scale top 50%
- Refresh losers with new angles

## When Fatigue is NOT the Problem

If frequency is low (< 2.0) but CPM is rising:
- **Auction pressure** — competitors are bidding up
- **Audience too narrow** — expand targeting
- **Seasonal spike** — Q4, holidays, industry events
- **Platform-wide CPM increase** — check Meta's ad auction trends

If frequency is high but CTR is stable/rising:
- **Audience saturation** — reach is plateauing
- **Need fresh audience** — not fresh creative
- **Expand targeting** — broader interests, lookalikes, or Advantage+

Always check frequency + CPM + CTR together. One metric alone doesn't tell the story.
